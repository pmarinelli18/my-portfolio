My name is Peyton Marinelli, I am currently pursuing a Bachelor of Science majoring in Computer Science Engineering at the University of Florida. Ever since I was a young child, I had a large interest in finding problems and creating solutions that would benefit the lives of people around me. In high school I joined student government and found that many problems that the school had could be resolved by improving the school website. This lead me down the road of web development and even further down the road to computer science.

Since then, my experience with computer science has grown exponentially. I have continuously been a contributing member in many clubs where I help develop projects and at the same time learn new skills. Within the Software Engineering Club, I helped contribute to a schoolwide application that would connect students and club organizations. Additionally, the Theme Park Engineering and Design Club has been working to build a small-scale working model of a roller coaster that we designed from scratch. My Job within this project has to develop the logic and software for how the coaster would operate. Other than Club-wide projects, I have also invested my time in projects outside of school.

In my first year of college I attended the Knights hack hackathon. For 36 hours straight I taught my self a couple new technologies I have never seen before although I had nothing to show for it. A few months later, I thought I could give it another try and actually attempt to complete something. At Shell Hacks my team and I put together a desktop application that used the twitter API to take in user accounts and based on their tweets would recommend their emotional status. Many people were interested in our project and we were happy how it came out. After this huge improvement from my first hackathon to my second, I decided to attend another and try to compete. At Hack FSU my team and I developed a desktop application that took in news articles and translated the content into a visual description using images. This Project ended up winning best use of Google Cloud and we could not have been happier.

I have had many great experiences relating to computer science and I am excited for what is next for me. This site will continue to be updated with the projects I am involved in. If you wish to get in contact with me my contact information could be found below.
